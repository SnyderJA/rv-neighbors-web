Arches National Park
Sun Outdoors Canyonlands Gateway, Moab UT  ·  May 8–15  ·  Full day off + 3 evening visits
Day off: Tue May 12
💡
Timed entry required Apr–Oct, 7am–4pm — book at recreation.gov NOW, morning slots sell out months ahead. On your day off (Tue May 12) grab an early timed entry. On other weekdays, you enter after 4pm — no reservation needed and crowds are thinning. The park has 2,000+ arches; Delicate Arch is the bucket-list centerpiece.
Fri
May 8
Work + 3pm
Work
Work until 2pm.
3pm · Enter
Enter Arches after 4pm — no reservation needed. First look at the park on the drive in.
Hike 1
Balanced Rock Loop (0.3 mi) — a 3,600-ton boulder balanced on a thin pedestal. Remarkable up close. Great golden-hour photos.
Hike 2
Double Arch (0.6 mi RT) — two massive arches you walk directly under. Shares a parking lot with Balanced Rock. One of the most dramatic spots in the park.
9pm

Tue
May 12
Day Off ✓
Full Day — Delicate Arch + Devils Garden + Fiery Furnace
Enter
Enter with a morning timed entry pass (book at recreation.gov well in advance). Arrive early to beat the heat and the crowds.
Scenic drive
Drive the full Arches Scenic Drive — stop at every pullout heading north. Courthouse Towers, La Sal Mountain Viewpoint, and the Windows section viewpoints. Stunning variety of formations.
Hike 1
Delicate Arch (3 mi RT, 629 ft elevation) — THE bucket-list hike. Walk across open slickrock to reach the world’s most iconic arch standing alone on the canyon rim. Allow 2–3 hours. Bring plenty of water — exposed trail with no shade.
Lunch break
Rest and eat at the Wolf Ranch picnic area near the Delicate Arch trailhead.
Hike 2
Landscape Arch (1.9 mi RT) at Devils Garden — the world’s longest natural arch at 306 ft. Flat, easy trail through a gorgeous canyon. Continue a short way further to see Navajo Arch and Partition Arch for bonus arches with almost no extra effort.
Hike 3
Sand Dune Arch (0.4 mi RT) — a beautiful arch tucked between fins with a sandy floor. Quick, easy, and totally unique. Kids and adults love it equally.
Late afternoon
Fiery Furnace Viewpoint — stop at the overlook to see this labyrinth of narrow sandstone fins that glows red at late-afternoon light. No permit needed for the viewpoint.
Sunset
Return toward the park entrance — Courthouse Towers and Three Gossips formations at golden hour are stunning. Pull off at the Park Avenue viewpoint for one final shot.
9pm
Back at RV by 9pm.

Bryce Canyon National Park
Day trip from Park City RV Resort  ·  Wed May 20  ·  2.5 hrs each way
Day off: Wed May 20
💡
Crowd tip: 60% of Bryce visitors come June–September. A Wednesday in late May is one of the quietest times of year. Elevation 8,000–9,100 ft — bring layers even in May. The 38-mile scenic drive alone is worth the trip.
Wed
May 20
Day Off ✓
Full Day — Bryce Scenic Drive + Navajo Loop + Bristlecone
Depart
Leave Park City at 7am — arrive Bryce ~9:30am. Wednesday means minimal crowds all day.
Scenic drive
Full 38-mile Bryce Scenic Drive — all 13 overlooks including Natural Bridge arch and Rainbow Point (9,115 ft, highest paved viewpoint in the park). Drive south to north.
Hike 1
Navajo Loop + Queens Garden Trail (2.9 mi RT) — the must-do hike. Wall Street narrow slot canyon, Thor’s Hammer hoodoo, and the surreal Queen’s Garden spires. Under 3 miles, moderate.
Hike 2
Bristlecone Loop (1 mi at Rainbow Point) — ancient pine forest, completely peaceful, sweeping views of the Grand Staircase stretching into the distance.
Drive home
Stop at Red Canyon on the way back — free, no crowds, stunning red fins right off the highway. Worth 20 minutes.
9pm
Back at RV by 9pm.

Before you go
Book-Ahead Reminders
🎫
Zion Timed Entry
Required Apr–Oct, 7am–4pm. Book at recreation.gov. Confirm RV height clearance for the Zion–Mt. Carmel Tunnel before arrival.
🏹
Arches Timed Entry — Book Now!
Required Apr–Oct, 7am–4pm. Morning slots for Tue May 12 sell out months ahead. Book at recreation.gov immediately. After 4pm entries are always open.
🌊
Homestead Crater
Geothermal soaking/snorkeling inside a volcanic crater in Midway, UT. Slots fill fast — book at homesteadresort.com before your trip.
🦎
Timpanogos Cave
Guided cave tours fill fast in May. Book at recreation.gov as early as possible. Plan for a 1.5-mile uphill hike to the entrance.
🚡
Deer Valley / PCMR Gondola
Lifts may not run mid-May for sightseers. Call Deer Valley or Park City Mountain Resort ahead to confirm May operating hours.
🍎
Gifford House Pie — Capitol Reef
Famous homemade pies in the historic Fruita district. Check hours at nps.gov/care before your day off on Wed May 6.
📱
Download Offline Maps
No cell service in Canyonlands, Capitol Reef, and many scenic byways. Download AllTrails and Google offline maps each morning before heading out.
🌡️
Layer Up for Bryce
Bryce Canyon sits at 8,000–9,100 ft. May mornings can be 30–40°F. Pack a jacket and layers for the Wed May 20 day trip.
💧
Water on Arches Day Off
Delicate Arch is a fully exposed 3-mile hike with zero shade and intense desert sun in May. Carry at least 3 liters of water per person and start early.
