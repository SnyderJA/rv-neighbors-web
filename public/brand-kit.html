01 — Overview

Who We Are

RV Neighbors is a community platform for the RV lifestyle — connecting travelers parked near each other, enabling families to travel together, and building a network of fellow road-dwellers. The brand is warm, outdoorsy, and grounded: deep forest greens meet golden amber, like a campfire at dusk under the pines.

🌲
Nature-Rooted
Forest greens and earth tones ground the palette in the outdoors. Every color choice should evoke open land and fresh air.
🔥
Warm & Welcoming
Amber accents bring the warmth of a campfire — approachable, human, and community-first in every interaction.
🚐
On the Road
Language and metaphor borrow from RV culture. We speak the language of travelers — casual, direct, and always moving forward.

06 — Brand

Brand Voice

RV Neighbors sounds like a knowledgeable fellow traveler — not a tech company. Casual, warm, direct. Use travel metaphors naturally. Write like you're talking to someone at the next campsite, not someone reading a manual.

✓ Do — Write like this
"You're all set! Let's get rolling and find your neighbors."
"4 fellow travelers are parked nearby. Tap to connect."
"Check in here to let neighbors know you've arrived."
"Looks like you took a wrong turn 🙃 — let's head back."
"Invite your family and hit the road together."
✕ Don't — Avoid this
"User registration has been completed successfully."
"There are 4 users within the geolocation radius."
"Please confirm your current GPS coordinates."
"An error has occurred. Please retry the operation."
"Utilize the family feature to add additional members."
Neighbor
Always use instead of "user", "member", or "contact" for connected people nearby.
Check In
The act of registering your current campground location. Two words, verb form.
On the Road
Universal metaphor for the RV lifestyle. Use to describe active/traveling status.
Connect
Sending a connection request. Always "Connect" not "Add", "Follow", or "Friend".
Fellow Traveler
Preferred descriptor for other app users, especially in onboarding and marketing.
Family
The sub-account system. "Family Members" not "Sub-users" or "Child accounts".
